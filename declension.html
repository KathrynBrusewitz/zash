Demonstrative Pronouns

Use demonstrative pronouns and adjectives to refer to something that has already been mentioned, or to point out a specific person or thing. Endings are the same as the definite article, except for the genitive and dative plural forms.

MNFPL
Nderdasdiedie
Adendasdiedie
Ddemdemderdenen
Gdessendessenderenderen

When referring to people, the demonstrative pronoun is equivalent to she, he, it, or they. When referring to things, it's equivalent to it, that, or those.

Use hier or da with a demonstrative to distinguish between this one or that one:

Der da gefällt Ryan besser.Ryan likes that one better.
Vergiss nicht, das hier zu drucken.Don't forget to print this one.

Der-Words

Der-words and their adjectives follow the same pattern of endings as definite articles.

Ein-Words

Ein-words and their adjectives follow the same pattern of endings as indefinite articles. Ein-words are all the possessive pronouns (mein, dein, sein...) and the negative kein.
